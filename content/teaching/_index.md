## Understanding Course Evaluations
____

#### Quantitative Evaluations

Quantitative data for course evaluations were collected via [REDCap](https://www.project-redcap.org/). Students submitted anonymous surveys at the middle and at the end of the semester. These data were in addition to the official evaluations.

Survey items were adapted from the University of California Berkeley [Center for Teaching & Learning](https://teaching.berkeley.edu/course-evaluations-question-bank), fully anchored on a Likert-type scale ranging from 1 (*Strongly Disagree*) to 7 (*Strongly Agree*). The only exception was survey item five, which was rated on a fully anchored, Likert-type scale ranging from 1 (*Very Poor*) to 7 (*Excellent*). The final survey item prompted an open-ended response.

**Survey Items**

1. The instructor presented content in an organized manner.
2. The instructor explained the concepts clearly.
3. The instructor was helpful when I had difficulties or questions
4. The instructor provided clear constructive feedback.
5. Considering both the limitations and possibilities of the subject matter and the course, how would you rate the overall effectiveness of this instructor?
6. What could the instructor improve, if anything?

#### Qualitative Evaluations

Qualitative data for course evaluations were collected by the Department Head anonymously and distributed to instructors after final grades were submitted.
